The Dell Partnership

Modernizing the Force: From Legacy Systems to Future-Ready Infrastructure

Over the past year, the Information Technology Bureau (ITB) has undertaken a massive, multi-faceted initiative to overhaul the technological backbone of the NYPD. By retiring end-of-life hardware and partnering with Dell, we have dramatically reduced costs, fortified our cyber resilience, and put faster tools in the hands of our officers.

Empowering the Frontline

The transformation begins where the technology meets the officer. For years, the department relied on OptiPlex 7010/7040 towers. While reliable, these systems were designed for an earlier era of computing. Officers frequently experienced significant downtime simply waiting for systems to boot and critical applications like the Domain Awareness System (DAS) to load.

In a recent pilot at the 47th Precinct, we replaced these legacy towers with the modern Dell Pro Max Slim, powered by Intel Core Ultra processors and DDR5 memory. The results were immediate and staggering.

Time to Productivity: 47th Precinct Pilot

Comparing legacy OptiPlex towers to the new Dell Pro Max Slim (measured in seconds).

Data summary: Legacy OptiPlex takes 260 seconds to boot, 37 seconds to load DAS, and 22 seconds to load cameras. The new Dell Pro Max Slim takes 12 seconds to boot, 7 seconds to load DAS, and 9 seconds to load cameras.

Boot times plummeted from over four minutes down to just 12 seconds. Loading a 6-camera feed in DAS dropped from 22 seconds to 9 seconds. Beyond speed, these new endpoints introduce a step-change in security. By moving to TPM 2.0 with SafeBIOS and utilizing self-encrypting NVMe storage, we are protecting sensitive law enforcement data against modern firmware-level threats.

"We are returning hundreds of hours of productivity back to our officers by eliminating the daily friction of waiting on legacy hardware."

Doing More With Less: The Datacenter

Behind the scenes, the infrastructure supporting critical applications like Facial Recognition, License Plate Readers (LPR), and the Real Time Crime Center (RTCC) was operating on a disparate combination of 5-to-10-year-old hardware. We were maintaining unsupported HPE Gen10 servers alongside isolated storage environments (VMAX, Unity, VNX).

Through the Data Center Refresh initiative, we are replacing this sprawling legacy footprint with centralized, high-end Dell PowerEdge 17G servers and PowerMax/Isilon storage arrays. This is a story of incredible consolidation.

Infrastructure Consolidation

Total Rack Units (RUs) required.

Data summary: Current Storage requires 1,341 Rack Units, future will require 739. Current Compute requires 1,404 Rack Units, future will require 758.

Compute Gains

The new servers provide 2.4x more CPU throughput using fewer sockets. By reducing our footprint by 11,230 cores, we drastically cut VMware licensing fees.

Environmental & Spatial Efficiency

We are freeing up 49 net datacenter racks and reducing power consumption by 70% (dropping from 120kW to just 36kW).

Security Upgrades & Financial Stewardship

Modernization isn't solely about performance; it is a vital necessity for cyber resilience. The new PowerMax architecture is STIG-hardened and FIPS 140 compliant. We are introducing immutable hardware roots of trust, secure data erasure, and Cyber Recovery Vaults with logical air-gapping. This directly protects NYPD data from sophisticated ransomware attacks.

Remarkably, this massive upgrade pays for itself in the long term. By partnering with Dell under a comprehensive Software Transformational License Agreement (TLA), we avoid the ballooning costs of maintaining legacy systems.
