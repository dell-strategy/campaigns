Executive Technology Transformation Roadmap

Technology availability at NYPD is inseparable from public safety. Fifteen months ago, the department faced increasing operational risk driven by aging, unsupported infrastructure. Today, through a decisive shift away from incremental remediation, we are rearchitecting our foundation to ensure a "Never Down" technology environment.

Empowering the Frontline

The transformation begins where the technology meets the officer. Across NYPD administration offices and commands in all five boroughs, many desktop systems were ten or more years old. Officers routinely experienced slow responsiveness and inconsistent performance. A lack of standardization meant multiple device models with differing specifications, creating uneven user experiences citywide and exposing the department to security vulnerabilities.

To eliminate this technology friction, NYPD completed a massive modernization initiative, deploying approximately 25,000 Dell Pro Slim Desktops and 50,000 monitors. This establishes a uniform, citywide experience regardless of borough or command.

The Modern Workstation Standard

  • Hardware: Latest Intel chipsets delivering dramatic performance improvements.
  • Displays: Dual 24-inch Dell monitors (one standard, one optimized for video conferencing).
  • Security: Windows Hello integration enabling fast, secure biometric authentication.
  • Collaboration: Full integration with Microsoft M365 (Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint).

Time to Productivity: 47th Precinct Pilot

Comparing legacy OptiPlex towers to the new Dell Pro Max Slim (measured in seconds).

The impact on the officer is profound. Login times have dropped from minutes to seconds—allowing officers to begin their shifts ready to work. Operationally, help desk tickets and downtime have declined significantly, freeing IT from break-fix activities.

"For NYPD leadership, uptime is not a traditional IT metric—it is a non-negotiable public safety requirement. NYPD must be 'Never Down'."

Mission Critical Data Foundations

The data foundation did not meet modern availability demands. Prior to modernization, storage platforms varied by organization (ITSD, DAS) and age, resulting in inconsistent architectures for file, block, and object workloads. This variability limited scalability, complicated support, and contributed to outages that affected officers both in the field and in command environments.

Today, NYPD operates on best-of-breed Dell storage platforms (PowerMax and Isilon) engineered for "five nines" (99.999%) availability. Systems such as the License Plate Reader (LPR), Domain Awareness System (DAS), and Arrest Processing now run faster and more reliably, restoring trust in data access.

Data Protection Built-In

NYPD standardized on Dell Data Domain as the enterprise platform protecting all data. Recovery is now reliable and predictable during incidents.

Security, Resilience, and Long-Term Stability

Every step of NYPD’s modernization has been built on a foundation of security. Over the last 15 months, NYPD has moved decisively from reactive recovery to engineered resilience. Aging, unsupported platforms have been replaced with modern technologies designed to withstand disruption and operate securely at scale.

Security has been embedded into the architecture rather than addressed as an afterthought. Furthermore, this massive upgrade pays for itself in the long term. By partnering with Dell under a comprehensive Software Transformational License Agreement (TLA), we avoid the ballooning costs of maintaining legacy systems and reduce power consumption by 70%.

Where We Are Going: The Road Ahead

While significant progress has been made across desktops, storage, and data protection, there are two major initiatives on the horizon essential to sustaining NYPD’s "Never Down" mission.

Compute Modernization

The current HPE server environment (Gen 8/9/10) no longer provides the scalability required for increasingly data-intensive applications.

NYPD has made the strategic decision to standardize on the latest Dell PowerEdge 17G servers powered by AMD processors. This provides a modern compute foundation for analytics and real-time data ingestion while reducing end-of-life support risks.

RMP Rugged Technology

A patrol car is the lifeblood of NYPD operations—the technology inside it must work every time. For years, the in-vehicle computing environment was powered by Panasonic rugged tablets that are now over a decade old.

NYPD is moving to Dell rugged in-vehicle technology. Designed to withstand vibration, heat, and extended usage, these devices will deliver faster access to information and a more reliable experience throughout an officer’s shift.

Partnership, Not Procurement

What is fundamentally different today is confidence. Fifteen months ago, technology posed risk. Today, it provides assurance.

Rather than approaching modernization as a series of isolated purchases, NYPD and Dell Technologies have worked together across planning, architecture, and execution. When technology is reliable, secure, and resilient, officers can focus fully on their mission. Public trust is strengthened.

When technology is never down, NYPD is always ready to serve New York City.
