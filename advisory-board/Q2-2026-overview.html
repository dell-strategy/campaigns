1. Whole-of-State Cyber Resilience & Funding

Context for this room: With Tony Sauerhoff (TX DIR) present, state-led initiatives like TX-RAMP and the distribution of the State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program (SLCGP) will be top of mind.

Dell Value Prop to mention: How Dell assists local entities in meeting state-mandated compliance frameworks quickly, and how our solutions map to grant-approved cybersecurity expenditures (MDR, Zero Trust infrastructure).

Target Audience:

2. Securing Critical Infrastructure & OT Convergence

Context for this room: Municipalities like Phoenix (water/heat infrastructure) and state agencies like TX Facilities Commission manage vast physical operations. The line between IT and OT (Operational Technology) is gone.

Dell Value Prop to mention: Dell's edge computing security, securing IoT deployments in smart cities, and segmenting IT/OT networks to prevent ransomware from jumping from administrative networks to physical infrastructure controls.

Target Audience:

3. Election Security & High-Profile Event Resilience

Context for this room: Counties typically manage election infrastructure. Furthermore, locations like Clark County (Las Vegas) manage massive influxes of people and high-profile events which are prime targets for disruption.

Dell Value Prop to mention: Immutable backups, rapid incident response retainers, and air-gapped recovery vaults (Cyber Recovery Solutions) to ensure continuity of operations no matter the threat.

Target Audience:

4. Generative AI Governance in Public Sector

Context for this room: Every SLG leader is facing pressure to adopt AI for citizen services while simultaneously terrified of data leakage, bias, and Shadow AI.

Dell Value Prop to mention: Dell's approach to "Bring AI to your data, not your data to AI." Securing on-premise or sovereign cloud AI deployments so cities and states maintain absolute custody of citizen data while leveraging LLMs.

Target Audience:
