Executive Context and Purpose

Technology availability at NYPD is inseparable from public safety.

At the outset of this transformation, the New York City Police Department faced increasing operational risk driven by aging, unsupported, and unreliable infrastructure. Core systems exceeded their expected lifecycle, and portions of the environment were no longer supported or supportable by vendors.

Frequent outages and performance degradation impacted mission critical policing applications, disrupting daily operations and reducing confidence in system reliability. What had once been tolerable became unacceptable.

Recognizing this, NYPD leadership made a decisive shift away from incremental remediation toward full modernization. The objective was clear: rearchitect the technology foundation to ensure continuous availability today while preparing for future operational growth.

To achieve this, NYPD engaged Dell Technologies as a strategic partner, not simply a vendor. The partnership encompassed architecture, planning, execution, and accountability, enabling NYPD to move toward a sustained transformation aligned with operational mission needs.

Definition of Executive Success

Success was defined clearly, decisively, and without ambiguity. For NYPD leadership, uptime was not a traditional IT metric—it was a non‑negotiable public safety requirement. System outages could directly affect officer effectiveness and citizen safety. Mission critical data centers were required to run on best-of-breed server, storage, networking, and data protection technologies, capable of continuous operation.

Certain systems were designated as always on, including the License Plate Reader (LPR), Domain Awareness System (DAS), and Arrest Processing platforms. Leadership knew the initiative succeeded when outages stopped impacting critical systems—and technology became invisible to operations.

"NYPD must operate in a Never Down technology environment."

The executive mandate that drove the decisive shift away from incremental remediation toward full modernization.

Where We Were: Baseline Conditions

Fifteen months ago, NYPD did not have the technology foundation required to fully support its mission of protecting the City of New York. Critical systems across the department were running on aging, end-of-life, and out of support technology. As reliance on technology increased, these conditions created unacceptable risk. Outages were happening more often, recovery took longer, and security exposure grew. Technology that NYPD depends on every day was no longer reliable enough to support modern policing.

Incremental fixes were no longer sufficient. Continuing to operate on outdated platforms meant greater disruption to operations, increased risk to data, and reduced confidence that systems would be available when officers and command staff needed them most. For an organization where technology supports investigations, real-time awareness, communications, and officer safety, this was not sustainable. Technology is not optional for NYPD—it is mission critical.

Leadership recognized that delaying action would only increase risk. The decision to modernize was necessary to restore stability, strengthen resilience, and ensure NYPD had the tools required to protect the city effectively. This transformation began because NYPD must be ready, resilient, and operational at all times.

Productivity Constraints

The desktop environment had become a productivity constraint rather than an enabler. Across NYPD administration offices and commands in all five boroughs, many desktop systems were 10 or more years old. Officers and staff routinely experienced long boot times, slow responsiveness, and inconsistent performance.

A lack of standardization created uneven experiences. Compounding this, many desktops could not be upgraded to Windows 11, exposing NYPD to a major Windows 10 security vulnerability. Technology friction directly reduced operational efficiency.

Data Foundation Risks

The data foundation did not meet modern availability demands. NYPD relied on storage platforms that were ten years old or older, not modernized for today’s performance and resiliency requirements.

Storage was distributed across multiple organizations, including ITSD and DAS, with inconsistent architectures. The resulting complexity increased operational overhead. Repeated outages eroded leadership confidence in data availability, performance, and recoverability.

Unsupported Infrastructure

Unsupported infrastructure introduced unacceptable operational and security risk. Several critical platforms—including Dell VNX storage and HPE Gen 8 and Gen 9 servers—had reached or exceeded end of vendor support.

In some cases, platforms were no longer supportable at all. This created challenges across uptime, incident recovery, and vendor escalation, forcing IT teams into a largely reactive operating model.

Desktop Environment Transformation

The desktop experience was rebuilt for speed, security, and consistency. NYPD completed a major modernization initiative by deploying Dell Pro Slim Desktops powered by the latest Intel chipset, delivering a dramatically improved end-user experience.

The new environment enables full integration with Microsoft M365, including Teams, OneDrive, and SharePoint, supporting modern collaboration across the department. Each workstation now includes Dual 24‑inch Dell monitors, one standard and one optimized for video conferencing with integrated webcam and speakers. Security is enhanced through Windows Hello, enabling fast, secure authentication.

Approximately 25,000 desktops and 50,000 monitors were deployed, creating a uniform citywide experience regardless of borough or command.

What This Means for NYPD: Login time has dropped from minutes to seconds—allowing officers to begin their shift ready to work. Operationally, help desk tickets and downtime have declined significantly, freeing IT from break fix activities and allowing focus on proactive enablement. Officers spend less time waiting on technology and more time performing their duties.

Storage Modernization

The data platform was rebuilt to support uninterrupted policing operations at city scale. NYPD has fully standardized and modernized its data center storage environment on Dell Technologies, establishing a mission critical foundation designed to keep essential policing systems continuously available.

Prior to modernization, storage platforms varied by organization and age. Today, NYPD operates on best-of-breed Dell storage platforms supporting file, block, and object workloads, protected by a modernized Dell Data Domain solution. This unified architecture simplifies operations while delivering the performance, scalability, and security required.

The new environment is engineered for five nines (99.999%) availability, ensuring that mission critical applications remain online. Systems such as the License Plate Reader, Domain Awareness System, Arrest Processing, and other operational platforms now run faster and more reliably.

What This Means for NYPD: Critical policing applications remain up and running without infrastructure driven interruptions. Command staff, officers, and investigators can rely on systems knowing the data layer supporting them is resilient, secure, and designed for continuous operation. NYPD can focus on protecting the City of New York—not managing storage outages.

Data Protection & Cyber Resilience

Data recoverability is now a dependable operational capability. For NYPD, data protection directly supports investigations, court proceedings, realtime operations, and officer safety. As policing has become increasingly data driven, confidence in the availability and integrity of that data has become essential to daily operations.

As part of the technology transformation, NYPD standardized on Dell Data Domain as the enterprise platform protecting all NYPD data across mission critical systems. This ensures critical information—from operational systems to investigative data—is consistently protected and recoverable.

Previously, data protection relied on aging platforms. Today, running Data Domain at department scale provides reliable, repeatable recovery, strengthens data integrity, and reduces exposure. Leadership has greater assurance that critical systems can be restored without uncertainty during outages.

"When incidents occur, the focus remains on policing—not on whether data can be recovered."

Partnership Impact

This transformation was enabled by partnership, not procurement. The partnership between NYPD and Dell Technologies has never been stronger. Through this collaboration, NYPD has moved to a fully standardized technology foundation, aligning on Dell solutions across desktops and laptops, storage, compute, data protection, and rugged tablets.

Rather than approaching modernization as a series of isolated purchases, NYPD and Dell worked together across planning, architecture, execution, and accountability. Standardization has been a critical outcome, simplifying operations, improving supportability, and strengthening system reliability across the department.

Most importantly, the partnership has remained focused on shared outcomes and NYPD’s mission, not individual products. The result is a resilient technology foundation that supports officers with the reliability required to perform their duties effectively.

Where We Are Going: The Road Ahead

Sustaining the 'Never Down' mission requires continuous momentum. Here is the roadmap for the next phase of modernization.

Compute Modernization (HPE → Dell)

Modern compute is the next critical step in sustaining NYPD’s “Never Down” mission.

While significant progress has been made across desktops, storage, and data protection, modernizing compute remains essential. The current HPE server environment has reached a point where it no longer provides the performance, scalability, or lifecycle assurance required. Much of the existing infrastructure is aging and approaching end of life, introducing unnecessary risk to mission critical systems.

To address these challenges, NYPD has made the strategic decision to move away from HPE and standardize on the latest Dell PowerEdge 17G servers powered by AMD processors. This aligns compute modernization with the broader standardization already achieved.

Dell PowerEdge 17G platforms deliver a modern compute foundation designed for high performance, efficiency, and scale. Powered by AMD chips, the new servers provide increased processing capability to support analytics, real-time data ingestion (like LPR and DAS), and future application demands, while also improving energy efficiency.

What This Means for NYPD: Critical applications will run on modern, fully supported compute platforms built to scale. Officers benefit from faster, more reliable systems, while IT gains a consistent, supportable environment that reduces operational risk.

Patrol Car / RMP Rugged Technology Modernization

A patrol car is the lifeblood of NYPD operations—technology inside it must work every time.

NYPD patrol vehicles are constantly in motion across the city, serving as the front line of public safety. These vehicles are mobile work environments that officers rely on for situational awareness, communication, reporting, and access to critical systems.

For years, the in vehicle computing environment was powered by Panasonic rugged tablets that are now more than a decade old. While durable in their time, these devices no longer meet the performance, reliability, or modern application requirements of today’s policing operations. Delays, freezes, or outages in a patrol car directly affect officer effectiveness and safety.

To address this, NYPD made the strategic decision to move away from Panasonic and modernize on Dell rugged in-vehicle technology. Dell rugged solutions are designed to withstand the physical demands of patrol work—vibration, heat, cold, and extended usage—while delivering modern performance.

What This Means for NYPD: Officers can rely on the technology in their patrol cars as confidently as the systems in command centers. Information is available when needed, systems respond quickly, and technology supports—rather than slows—field operations.

Security, Resilience, and Long Term Stability

Every step of NYPD’s modernization has been built on a foundation of security, resilience, and long term stability. Across desktops, storage, compute, data protection, and in-vehicle technology, NYPD’s transformation has not focused solely on modernization for modernization’s sake. Each decision has been guided by a commitment to protecting systems, safeguarding data, and ensuring continuous operations.

Over the last 15 months, NYPD has moved decisively from reactive recovery to engineered resilience. Security has been embedded into the architecture rather than addressed as an afterthought. Standardized platforms, modern operating systems, supported hardware, and consistent configurations have strengthened NYPD’s security posture while simplifying oversight.

NYPD is better positioned to move forward with confidence. Technology now supports the department’s ability to protect the City of New York without introducing unnecessary risk or instability.
