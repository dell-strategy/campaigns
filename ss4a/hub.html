What is SS4A? (And Why Act Now)

The Safe Streets and Roads for All (SS4A) is a $5 billion program (FY22-26). FY26 is the final year of funding under the current authorization. It funds local initiatives to prevent roadway deaths ("Vision Zero").

1. Planning & Demonstration Grants

For customers who need to build or update a Comprehensive Safety Action Plan. Includes funding for feasibility studies, pilot programs, and data collection.

  • ✓ Up to $5 Million per grant
  • ✓ Feasibility studies for new tech (e.g., adaptive signals)
  • ✓ Developing holistic safety roadmaps

2. Implementation Grants

For customers who already have an Action Plan and are ready to deploy infrastructure, behavioral, or operational activities.

  • ✓ Up to $25 Million per grant
  • ✓ Deploying intelligent transportation systems (ITS)
  • ✓ Upgrades to public safety radio & dispatch systems

Target Segments & Personas

Eligible applicants include Counties, Cities, MPOs, and Tribal Governments. K-12 and Higher-Ed often partner with these entities.

Cities & Towns

Prime targets for Smart Intersections and Vision Zero initiatives.

Target Personas:

  • CIO / CTO
  • Director of Transportation
  • Public Works Director

Counties & MPOs

Focus on regional coordination, rural roadway safety, and data sharing.

Target Personas:

  • County IT Director
  • MPO Planners
  • Sheriff / EMS Leaders

K-12 Districts

Must partner with local govt. Focus: Safe Routes to School, crosswalk beacons.

Target Personas:

  • Superintendent
  • Director of Safety
  • Transportation Director

Higher Education

Must partner with local govt. Focus: Smart campus, pedestrian safety.

Target Personas:

  • VP Admin / Finance
  • Campus Safety Director
  • Smart Campus Lead
