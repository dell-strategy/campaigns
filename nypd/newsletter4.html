01 Storage Modernization

Forty arrays, four platforms, one simplified core.

For a decade, NYPD's most mission-critical applications — DAS, Arrest Processing, LPR, AVL, RTCC — ran across a sprawl of VMAX, Unity, VNX, XtremIO, Isilon, Data Domain, and fibre-channel directors, each with its own management plane, capacity ceiling, and security posture. The Dell TLA refresh consolidates roughly 40% of that environment that is past useful life onto a high-end PowerMax, Isilon, and Data Domain core — built for Zero Trust, sized for the next decade.

Then · 5–10 yrs

The Sprawl

  • 5 VMAX arrays (purchased 2015)
  • 11 Unity arrays (2019–2020)
  • 6 VNX arrays (≤ 2018)
  • 8 XtremIO arrays (2015)
  • 4 Isilon · 2 Data Domain · 4 FC directors
  • Multiple end-of-support dates already passed
Now · In motion

The Refresh

  • 3 PowerMax + 1 PowerMax expansion
  • 8 Isilon arrays
  • 4 Data Domain + 2 expansions
  • 2 Fibre channel directors
  • Bundled 5-year support, fixed-price maintenance
  • 14-month implementation underway
Next · Future state

The Posture

  • Zero Trust capable, STIG-hardened, FIPS 140
  • Immutable hardware root of trust
  • AI-driven anomaly & ransomware detection
  • Cyber recovery vaults · air-gap isolation
  • > 99.9999% data availability SLA
  • Headroom for analytics, AI, video evidence

02 Compute Transformation

The largest compute refresh in NYPD history.

More than 380 server hosts across four data centers — the workhorses behind every DAS query, every license-plate read, every dispatch — are being modernized and consolidated onto Dell PowerEdge 17G. The result: a denser, faster, more secure platform with the headroom NYPD will need for AI-driven analytics, while reclaiming racks, cooling, and dollars in the process.

4×
PCIe Bandwidth
PCIe Gen5 unlocks the next generation of NICs, NVMe, and accelerators on a single platform.

04 RMP Rugged Tablet Refresh

The patrol car gets a new dashboard.

A decade is a long time in the life of a tablet. The current in-vehicle units are approaching ten years old and no longer match modern OS requirements, performance demands, or the realities of life on patrol. The new generation rugged tablet — paired with an upgraded vehicle mount — modernizes the most-used computer in the Department.

→

03
CPU · GPU · NPU headroom Substantial gains across all three — built for the next wave of edge analytics and AI-enabled field tools.
04
Upgraded vehicle mount Better positioning and flexibility, designed to reduce the injury risk associated with older in-vehicle tray configurations.

05 Field Notes · Quick Wins

The work between the capital projects.

Alongside the headline refreshes, NYPD and Dell residents are running a backlog of low-cost, high-impact "quick wins" surfaced during the joint Technical Assessment. Most cost zero dollars and take 1–6 months. They're organized around four operating principles — Never Down, Stability, Simplification & Autonomy, and Management — and they keep the environment hardened while the bigger transformations land.

i.

Never Down

— Resilience & Continuity
  • Cross-replication of VM backups across DR Data Domains
  • Increase Fibre Channel ports — separate production & backup traffic
  • Migrate remaining VMAX into PowerMax
  • Apply Cyber Security features in CloudIQ
  • Superna auditing & ransomware protection on PowerScale
Cost: $0
ii.

Stability

— Hardening What's Live
  • Stabilize Computer Crimes backups on Gen5A
  • Validate config with small-share test before scaling
  • Work forward from 2003 on DD1, backward from 2025 on DD2
  • Upgrade core PowerScale clusters to OneFS 9.10.x
Cost: $0
iii.

Simplify & Automate

— Less Toil, More Signal
  • Evaluate SQL backups with PPDM
  • Agent install on SQL servers, configure backup policy
  • Run backups, record performance with DBA team
  • Evaluate restore functionality before scaling
Cost: $0
iv.

Management

— Single Pane of Glass
  • DAS update of DCNM
  • Install & configure centralized NDFC instance
  • One management plane across all DAS SAN fabrics
  • Reduce per-fabric tooling overhead
Cost: $0
