The Executive Mandate

Technology availability at NYPD is inseparable from public safety.

At the outset of this transformation, the New York City Police Department faced increasing operational risk driven by aging, unsupported, and unreliable infrastructure. Core systems had exceeded their expected lifecycle.

Frequent outages and performance degradation impacted mission-critical policing applications, disrupting daily operations and reducing confidence. What had once been tolerable became unacceptable. Leadership made a decisive shift away from incremental remediation toward full modernization.

"NYPD must operate in a Never Down technology environment."

Uptime is not a traditional IT metric—it is a non‑negotiable public safety requirement.

Where We Were: Baseline Conditions

Fifteen months ago, critical systems across the department were running on aging, end-of-life, and out-of-support technology.

Productivity Constraints

Desktops across five boroughs were 10+ years old. Officers experienced long boot times, slow responsiveness, and a severe Windows 10 security vulnerability, drastically reducing operational efficiency.

Data Foundation Risks

Storage platforms were 10+ years old, distributed unevenly across ITSD and DAS. Complexity increased operational overhead, recovery risk, and caused repeated outages affecting command environments.

Unsupported Infrastructure

Critical platforms (Dell VNX, HPE Gen 8/9) exceeded vendor support. This forced IT into a highly reactive break-fix model, creating unacceptable operational and security risks.

Desktop Environment Transformation

The desktop experience was rebuilt for speed, security, and consistency. NYPD deployed Dell Pro Slim Desktops, enabling full integration with Microsoft M365 (Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint).

Each workstation includes dual 24‑inch monitors, optimized for video conferencing. Security is enhanced through Windows Hello for fast, secure authentication.

Officer Impact: Officers spend less time waiting on technology and more time performing their duties. Help desk tickets and downtime have declined significantly.

Storage & Cyber Resilience

The data platform was rebuilt to support uninterrupted policing operations at city scale. NYPD has standardized on best-of-breed Dell storage platforms (file, block, object workloads) and protected them with Dell Data Domain.

Crucial systems—License Plate Reader (LPR), Domain Awareness System (DAS), and Arrest Processing—now run faster and reliably. Data recoverability is a dependable operational capability, ensuring investigations and court proceedings are never delayed by IT.

IT Impact: Simplified operations, predictable recovery, and a secure foundation capable of supporting future data growth. The focus remains on policing, not recovering data.

Where We Are Going: The Road Ahead

Sustaining the 'Never Down' mission requires continuous momentum. Here is the roadmap for the next phase of modernization.

Compute Modernization (HPE → Dell)

Moving away from aging HPE servers to the latest Dell PowerEdge 17G servers powered by AMD processors. This aligns compute with the broader standardization achieved across the department.

  • Supports data-intensive, real-time analytics (LPR, DAS)
  • Reduces end-of-life and support risk
  • Improved energy efficiency and scaling

Patrol Car Rugged Tech (Panasonic → Dell)

A patrol car is the lifeblood of operations. We are replacing 10-year-old Panasonic tablets with Dell rugged in-vehicle technology designed to withstand the physical demands of patrol work.

  • Faster access to critical field information
  • Withstands vibration, heat, and extended usage
  • Predictable lifecycle management & support
